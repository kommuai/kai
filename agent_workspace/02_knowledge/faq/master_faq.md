## What software is KommuAssist running?

KommuAssist houses the open-source software openpilot.

## What openpilot version is it running?

We do not always use the most updated version of openpilot, we will only update the software when we have deemed the latest version stable to be used on local roads. KommuAssist2 has two different modes, based on the distance button set, it has chill mode (3 bar distance), normal mode (2 bar distance) and aggressive mode (1 bar distance). Depending on which mode, the driving model behaves differently. The aggressive mode will feel like just a normal adaptive cruise control that follows the vehicle closely, it will not stop for speed bumps and traffic lights. Normal mode drives more relaxed below 30km/h and drives more closely to the lead car above 30km/h. It is normal for chill mode to not accelerate up to the user’s set speed. Both normal mode and chill mode will slow down for curves, and it will stop for red light and speed bumps only below 30km/h. However, it will not be able to handle complicated traffic scenarios like roundabouts, u-turn and junction turning. KommuAssist2 is designed from ground up and all the parts are locally manufactured here in Malaysia. KommuAssist2 will include turn-by-turn navigation in the future, but it will only be rolled out after the basic functions are stable.

## What is rent-to-own? What are the terms?

Kommu’s Rent-to-own plan is a 3-year rental tenure where the device remains Kommu’s property. Upon the expiry of the 3-year rental period, Customer shall continue to use the Product without rental payment to Kommu, and ownership of the Product will be transferred to the Customer. Installation happens after credit checks and payment of an Initial Deposit equal to 12 months of the Monthly Rental Fee (MRF). A 10-day cooling-off period applies before installation; after expiry of the 3-year term, rental payments stop and Product warranty ends. The deposit is applied to the final 12 months of the 36-month term, not applicable to early or missed payments, and—once installed—is non-refundable. Payments are via auto-debit of credit card only; if a payment fails, Kommu may retry charges, report to credit agencies, and pursue collections.

## What is authorise remote support? How can I allow remote support?

Remote support is a function that allows a Kommu technical staff to remote into the device to help with emergency diagnosis of the device. The remote support can be activated by going to the KommuAI app and pressing on “authorize remote support”. Make sure the KommuAssist2 device has an active internet connection. ## What is the maximum speed it can function?

KommuAssist usually follows the original ACC’s max speed. That means if your car’s maximum is 150km/h, it can be used up to 150km/h. A special case is given to only Perodua vehicles where the max set speed has been bumped up to 140km/h.

## Is this approved by JPJ or by the Ministry of Transport MOT? Is this legal?

Please check with your local authorities if you are not within Malaysia. JPJ or Ministry of Transport Malaysia does not have any regulatory framework on aftermarket ADAS. So that means we are technically not regulated and being used in a grey area. However, we are one of the committee members of Jawatankuasa Autonomous Vehicle that comprises JPJ, MARii, MiROS and MOT and is adhering to the guidelines set within the committee on the testing framework.
